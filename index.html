The problem

Managing field operations manually is costing you

Every unresolved fault is an SLA clock ticking. Every missed installation is a frustrated customer. Without a system, things fall through the cracks.

Delayed fault resolution

WhatsApp groups and phone calls slow everything down. By the time a technician is dispatched the SLA clock has already been running for an hour — and the penalty is getting closer.

Poor SLA compliance

Without real-time visibility, operations managers have no way to know which jobs are about to breach their SLA deadline until it is already too late to act.

No visibility over your field team

How many open jobs does each technician have right now? Who is available? Without a system nobody knows — and the wrong person gets dispatched to the wrong job.

Lost revenue and repeat faults

SLA penalties hit your bottom line every month. Repeat faults at the same node go unnoticed because there is no fault history. The same problems keep coming back.

How it works

From job created to job closed in three steps

FibreOps replaces the WhatsApp groups, spreadsheets and phone calls with one streamlined workflow that works for every job type.

1

Job is created

The operations manager logs a job in FibreOps — fault, installation, maintenance or upgrade. They assign a technician, set the SLA deadline, and the clock starts.

2

Technician is dispatched

The assigned technician receives the job instantly on their mobile app with full details — location, fault type, SLA deadline, and any notes from the manager.

3

Job closed and recorded

The technician completes the job, uploads photos or OTDR traces, and marks it done. A completion report is generated automatically and the SLA outcome is recorded.
